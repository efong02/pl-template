Completing the square is a technique for finding the roots of an expression. Its name comes from the process of adding a well-chosen constant to both sides of an expression of the form $x^2 - bx = c$ such that the left hand side simplifies to the form $(x - \frac{b}{2})^2$. The well-chosen constant for expressions of the form $x^2-bx = c$ is precisely $\frac{b^2}{4}$.

The end behavior of a function $f(x)$ is how the function behaves as $x$ gets very large or very negative. We describe that symbolically as $\lim_{x \to \infty} f(x)$ and $\lim_{x \to -\infty} f(x)$, respectively.

If either $\lim_{x \to \infty}$ or $\lim_{x \to -\infty} f(x)$ is finite, then we say that $f(x)$ has a horizontal asymptote at $y = \lim_{x \to \infty} f(x)$ or $y = \lim_{x \to -\infty} f(x)$, respectively. Sometimes these limits are different, but for the problem below, they are the same.
